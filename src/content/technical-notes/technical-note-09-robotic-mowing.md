## Looking beyond the purchase price

Golf Greens Committees are asking the question: "Will robotic mowing save money?"

It's a perfectly reasonable question, but it isn't the most useful one. The better question is:
"What is the overall business case for our golf club?"

The purchase price of robotic mowers is easy to compare with conventional machinery, and estimating
annual running costs is usually straightforward. However, some of the most significant benefits of
robotic mowing are much more difficult to express in pounds and pence.

Improved winter playability, an extended effective playing season, less damage to wet fairways, and
more time for course improvement work may ultimately prove more valuable than the direct financial
savings.

This Technical Note explains a practical approach to assessing both the measurable costs and the
wider operational benefits of robotic fairway mowing.

## Why this calculator was developed

Golf clubs are increasingly interested in robotic mowing, but many struggle to compare it fairly
with conventional mowing. Some focus almost entirely on the purchase price. Others look only at
labour savings. Neither approach tells the whole story.

The Golf Robot Business Case Calculator has therefore been developed to help clubs compare the
measurable annual cash costs of conventional and robotic mowing, while recognising that many
important operational benefits cannot easily be expressed in financial terms.

It is intended to support informed discussion by Greens Committees, Course Managers, and Treasurers,
rather than promote any particular manufacturer or system. In this early phase of robotic mower
development, it is likely that many clubs will choose to retain one or more conventional mowers and
that robots will be part of a "hybrid" system, which is what the Calculator assumes.

## A good business case has two parts

Every business case should consider both measurable financial information and the wider operational
benefits.

### Measured annual cash costs

These can usually be estimated with reasonable confidence:

- Machinery purchase and replacement costs
- Fuel and electricity
- Maintenance and servicing
- Labour costs associated with fairway mowing
- Annual operating costs

These calculations provide an objective comparison of the annual cash costs of conventional and
robotic mowing.

### Additional greenkeeper capacity

The calculator also estimates the amount of skilled greenkeeper capacity released by robotic
mowing. This is not treated as a financial saving, but is shown separately, because it represents
valuable time that can be redirected towards course improvement work such as drainage improvements,
bunker maintenance, tree and woodland management, irrigation improvements, and course presentation.

## Benefits not included in the cash cost comparison

Many of the most valuable benefits vary from club to club and therefore cannot be calculated
reliably. These may include:

- Improved winter playability
- Potential to extend the effective playing season
- Reduced soil compaction and rutting
- Ability to maintain wetter areas throughout the winter
- Better grass control entering the main growing season
- Reduced diesel consumption and emissions
- Improved member satisfaction
- Potential for increased visitor golf and green fee income

A good business case considers all the potential benefits, not just costs.

## Where do the real benefits come from?

Much of the discussion surrounding robotic mowing concentrates on labour savings. In practice, the
greatest long-term benefits may come from something quite different.

Lightweight robotic mowers can often continue working when conventional ride-on mowers would risk
damaging saturated ground. This helps keep grass under control throughout the winter, reducing
spring recovery work and improving the presentation of wet areas.

Perhaps more importantly, robotic mowing releases skilled greenkeeper capacity for work that
directly improves the golf course. This may include:

- Drainage improvements
- Bunker maintenance
- Tree and woodland management
- Irrigation improvements
- Tee and approach improvements
- Course presentation
- Environmental improvement projects

These activities often improve the golf course for many years, whereas routine mowing simply
maintains it.

## Using the Business Case Calculator

The calculator has been designed to be as simple as possible. Most clubs only need to enter:

- Total fairway area
- Percentage to be mown robotically
- Hours currently required to mow all fairways
- Typical annual mowing period
- Greenkeeper hourly employment cost
- Machinery costs

The calculator then estimates:

- Robotic mower requirements
- Annual operating costs
- Annual cash savings
- Additional greenkeeper capacity released
- Equivalent annual value of that capacity

It also produces a committee-ready Golf Robotic Mowing Business Case summarising the results.

## Final thoughts

No calculator can make a decision for a golf club. It can only provide reliable information on those
costs and savings that can reasonably be measured.

Many of the wider operational benefits discussed in this Technical Note will vary according to local
soils, drainage, climate, course layout, and management priorities. For some clubs, these wider
benefits may ultimately prove more valuable than the direct annual cash saving itself.

### The bottom line

The calculator measures what can reasonably be measured. The Committee must then consider the wider
benefits that cannot easily be expressed in financial terms.

Only by considering both can the overall business case for robotic mowing be properly assessed.
